About

Hey there! I'm Abhisek, a computer science enthusiast with a passion for pushing the boundaries of AI and robotics. I earned my master's degree in computer science from McGill University, where I had the privilege of studying under the guidance of Professor Gregory Dudek. My research focused on leveraging inverse reinforcement learning for socially compliant navigation, delving into the fascinating intersection of artificial intelligence and human interaction.

Following my graduation, I embarked on an exciting journey as a research engineer at Samsung AI Center, Montreal. At Samsung, I had the opportunity to collaborate on a diverse array of projects spanning robotics, telecommunications, and smart home automation.

Beyond the world of algorithms and experiments, I find joy in artistic pursuits. Whether it's sketching intricate designs or painting vibrant landscapes, creativity fuels my downtime. When the weather permits, you'll often find me exploring the great outdoors on my trusty bike, soaking in the sights and sounds of nature.

I'm driven by a curiosity to unravel the mysteries of technology and a desire to make meaningful contributions to society. Let's connect and explore the endless possibilities together!

Abstract Paper link

Learning goal conditioned socially compliant navigation from demonstration using risk-based features.

Abhisek Konar, Bobak Hamed Baghi, Gregory Dudek.

IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, 2021

In this work, we aim to learn socially compliant navigation patterns from demonstrations. We use inverse RL to learn a reward function and propose a novel risk-based features that effectively captures the navigational implications of a given snapshot of a crowd. Our proposed method produces trajectories that reach the goal while exhibiting social compliance.

Abstract Paper link

SESNO: Sample Efficient Social Navigation from Observation

Bobak Hamed Baghi, Abhisek Konar, Francois Hogan, Michael Jenkin, Gregory Dudek.

IEEE International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems, 2022

Building on the previous work, this paper introduces SESNO: a new sample efficient algorithm for social navigation. SESNO improves on previous methods by reusing past experiences to learn faster. Testing it with pedestrian data sets shows that SESNO outperforms other methods while needing much fewer samples to learn, making it more efficient.

Abstract Paper link

Working Backwards: Learning to Place by Picking

Oliver Limoyo, Abhisek Konar, Trevor Ablett, Jonathan Kelly, Francois R Hogan, Gregory Dudek

arXiv preprint, 2024

Collecting demonstrations for robot learning is tedious. To eschew that, we propose a method to autonomously collect real-world demonstrations for a family of placing tasks. Banking on the rather simple idea: placing is the inverse of picking; we propose a system that can reliably collect hundreds of demonstrations without the need for human intervention. We use a combination of existing grasping algorithms, combined with compliant control and tactile sensing for regrasping. Our experiments show that training policies using data from our approach are better than their counterparts trained from kinesthetic teaching data.

Abstract Paper link

Communication Load Balancing via Efficient Inverse Reinforcement Learning

Abhisek Konar, Di Wu, Yi Tian Xu, Seowoo Jang, Steve Liu, Gregory Dudek

IEEE International Conference on Communications, 2023

In this work, we explore the use of inverse RL(IRL) in communication load balancing, a novel approach in this field. Like most real-world problems, crafting a reward function that well and truly defines the problem is hard. So, we use IRL to learn a reward function from ranked demonstrations. This further eleminates the need for 'expert' demonstrations, another requirement for IRL methods. Experimental results on simulated traffic scenarios demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method compared to existing baselines, achieving superior performance by a significant margin.

Abstract Paper link

Mixed-variable PSO with fairness on multi-objective field data replication in wireless networks

Dun Yuan, Yujin Nam, Amal Feriani, Abhisek Konar, Di Wu, Seowoo Jang, Xue Liu, Gregory Dudek

IEEE International Conference on Communications, 2023

This paper explores the use of digital twins to support wireless network development, focusing on replicating field data in simulation accurately. Unlike prior approaches, we proposes a data-driven search method using mixed-variable particle swarm optimization (PSO) to find optimal simulation parameters. Additionally, it addresses conflicts between key performance indicators (KPIs) by adjusting their importance during the search. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach in improving replication accuracy, enhancing fairness between KPIs, and achieving faster convergence compared to other methods.

AIoT Smart Home via Autonomous LLM Agents

Dmitriy Rivkin, Francois Hogan, Amal Feriani, Abhisek Konar, Adam Sigal, Xue Liu, Gregory Dudek

in submission, IEEE IoT Journal, 2024

Following the boom in Large language models(LLMs), we leverage the versatility of these models in a smart home setting to create a system that add vastly augments the abilities of existing smart home assistants. In this work, we present SAGE (Smart home Agent with Grounded Execution), a smarthome assistant that can understand spatial device reference, provide a personalized assistance and follow through complex chains of command.
